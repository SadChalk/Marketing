Intro to Marketing Notes (Ch.1)

1-1 What Is Marketing?

General Ideas
  • Marketing is internal and external
  • Focus on customer's needs, not the business' -- though the two go hand-in-hand.
  • Major focus is understanding the needs and wants of customers
  • Marketing is the connection between business and consumer -- find what the customer wants, improve product, promote product
  • Offer the customer what they are looking for before another company gets on board
Marketing Concept
  • Focus on customer wants and needs so that the organization can distinguish its offerings from those of its competitors.
  • Integrate all the organization's activities, including production, to satisfy customer wants.
  • Achieve long-term goals for the organization by satisfying customer's wants and needs legally and responsibly.

1-2 The History of Marketing

The Production Era (Henry Ford and earlier)
  • Focuses on products because of a lack of options in the marketplace; thrives from a lack of competition
  • "If you build it, they will come"
  • Marketing was a messenger
The Sales Era (Door-to-door Salesmen)
  • Consumer choice
  • Convince customers to buy
  • The focus is on the hard sell
The Marketing Company Era (Mad Men)
  • Coordination of Marketing activities
  • Customers are grouped into segments based on demographics.
  • Marketers work to understand the segments
  • Segments become targets -- marketing focuses. Target market should be accurately defined.
  • Persuasion and marketing become intermixed; using emotional connections
  • Rifle (narrow $) vs Shotgun (broad $$$) marketing approach
  • Consumers are more shrewd; "I know you are trying to sell this to me".
The Societal Marketing Era ("Save the Environment")
  • What if giving what the consumer wants is actually not good for the consumer or for the environment?
  • New movements have resulted in recycling, waste reduction, and safety.
  • Health issues are at the forefront (Cigarettes, caloric info at restaurants)
The Relationship Marketing Era ("Join the Family")
  • From interruption to interaction
  • Long-term engagement is the focus
  • Two new customer-based strategies encourage the engagement (Customer satisfaction, Relationship marketing)
  • Develop trust with the customer
  • Give full attention; don't let the relationship lapse; monitor and engage

Customer Relationship Management

  • Ultimate goal to understand each customer individually while ultimately meeting needs of all customers and building relationships
  • Build trust -- listen to the customer, share stories/testimonials, act upon customer desires
  • Interaction every day of the year... 24 hours a day --- companies can create relationships with customer through social and digital media and have a dialogue every day of the year
Exchange
  • People giving up one thing to receive another that they would rather have. An exchange can take place only if the following five conditions exist:
  1. At least two parties are involved
  2. Each party has something that ay be of value to the other party
  3. Each party is capable of communication and delivery
  4. Each party is free to accept or reject the exchange offer
  5. Each party believes it is appropriate or desirable to deal with the other party.
Customer Value
  • People giving up one thing to receive another that they would rather have.
  • Marketers interested in customer value offer products that perform, earn trust through loyalty programs, avoid unrealistic pricing by communicating clearly, give consumers the facts and the opportunity to learn more, offer an organization-wide commitment to service and after-sales support, and partner with consumers to cocreate experiences that consumers want.

The Marketing Mix (The four 'P's)
  • Product --- the content and container of what is being sold
  • Price --- how much the consumer gives to get the product
  • Place --- how the product gets from where it's made to where it's sold
  • Promotion --- how the message of the product gets to the consumer
Example: Rice Krispies
  • Product --- flavour, texture, nutritional content, packaging design, box size, labelling
  • Price --- attractive price to consumer and make $$
  • Place --- transporting the wheat and selling the final product, where to sell it -- Walmart, Amazon -- and the to consumers
  • Promotion --- raising awareness
  • Overall goal --- lead to satisfaction and a long-term relationship -- maybe using the cereal in the future
