3-1 The Importance Of Strategic Planning

General Ideas
  • Leadership and managerial process of establishing organizational objectives and determining how to achieve them
  • Internal resources
  • Marketing environment - all of the conditions internally (strengths and weaknesses) and externally (opportunities and threats) in which a business operates
Strategic Planning--3 levels

Corporate

  • Sets the direction and scope--mission statement, business opportunities, and resource allocation

Business

  • Takes place at the strategic business unit (SBU) level
  • The goal is to gain a competitive advantage

Marketing

  • Target marketing and marketing mix activities

3-2 Strategic Business Units

Strategic vs. Tactical Decisions
Strategic -- "The Big Picture" - Made at all three levels
- Wider in scope
- Longer in orientation
- Affect long-run course, allocation of resources, and financial success
Tactical -- "The Front Line" - Made at the operational or marketing level
- Narrow in scope
- Short-term
- Limited impact of long-run profitability
- Directed from strategic decisions made at the corporate level

Business Planning for Competitive Advantage

How to implement and execute the strategy for growth for the SBU:

  • Complete a SWOT analysis to achieve a sustainable competitive advantage and identify core competencies.

3-3 Competitive Advantage

  • The set of unique features of a company and its products that are perceived by the target market as significant and superior to the competition.
  • Cost
  • Product / service differentiation
  • Niche strategies
Cost Competitive Advantage

Costs can be reduced in different ways:

  • Experience curves: demonstrate that costs decline at a predictable rate as experience with a product increases. Allows companies to predict what costs will be so they can manage long-term.
  • Efficient labour: labour costs can be a major component in labour intensive industries. Solutions include: off shore.
  • No-frills goods and services: remove frills or options from a product or service
  • Government subsidies: provide grants and interest-free loans to target industries
  • Product design: reverse engineering to understand all components or processes and reduce costs in this way, Ex. Wonderbread - bread bags, Uber and Toyota - driverless cars - increase revenues by 70%
  • Production innovation: new tech, simpler production, will lower cost of production. CAD and CAM reduce costs.
  • New methods of service delivery: paperless banks, Tangerine cafes and pop-up kiosks

BUT costs can only go so low...

Product Differentiation Competitive Advantage

Unique Benefit + Of value to customer = Product differentiation

Product / service differentiation provides a longer-lasting competitive advantage than price.

Niche Competitive Advantage
  • Serving the needs of a single segment that has good growth potential but is not crucial to the success of the major competitors

Niches can be geography or product-based.

Building a Sustainable Competitive Advantage
  • An advantage that
  • Cannot be copied
  • Can be sustained
  • Sources of tomorrow's competitive advantages
  • Skills and assets of the organization
  • Marketing leaders need to continually focus on these skills and assets to create and sustain the competitive advantage.

3-4 Marketing Strategy

General Idea

The activities of selecting and describing one or more target markets and developing and maintaining a marketing mix that will produce mutually satisfying exchanges with target markets

Setting Marketing Plan Objectives

Marketing objective - the statement of what will be accomplished through marketing activities

  • Specific - no generalities
  • Measurable - units, $
  • Achievable - attainable
  • Realistic - relevant
  • Time-bound - time frame

Poorly Stated Objectives

  • Our objective is to be a leader in the industry in new product development
  • Out objective is to maximize profits

SMART Objectives

  • Out objective is to spend 12 percent of sales revenue between 2021 and 2022 on research and development in an effort to introduce at least five new products in 2023.
Target Market Strategy
  • Begins with an MOA = market opportunity analysis
  • Estimation of size and sales potential of segments that are of interest
  • Then a decision regarding which/how many segments to target
  • Target market(s)
  • Single segment
  • Entire market
  • Multiple segments

General Idea

  • Sales promotion
  • Personal selling
  • Digital marketing

3-6 Marketing Plan Implementation, Evaluation, and Control

Implementation
  • The process that turns the plan into action--"doing what you said you were going to do"
  • Can be a source of failure
Evaluation \& Control
Evaluation Control Marketing Audit
Have marketing objectives been achieved over period of time?

Objectives fail because:
- The objective was unrealistic
- Inappropriate marketing strategies in a plan
- Poor implementation
- Changes in environment after the strategy is implemented		 - Mechanism to evaluate and take corrective action
- Informal and formal controls create efficiency		 Four characteristics:

- Comprehensive--cover all marketing issues, not just challenge areas
- Systematic--orderly sequence analysis of marketing environment, internal marketing system, and activities.
- Independent--someone who can be objective in analysis.
- Periodic--completed regularly
