Step 3: Specify the sampling procedure

Can't talk to everyone, so must select a subset of the population -- a sample.

  1. Population of interest must be defined - people whose opinions, behaviours, preferences, attitudes matter to the marketer. Ex. Snapchat - all current users of Snapchat, maybe likely users of other applications like TikTok.

  2. Must the sample be representative of the population?

    i Yes: Probability sample represents the population

    ii No: Nonprobability sample does not represent a cross-section of the population

3. Types of errors that can occur must be acknowledged.

  • Measurement error: Information provided by the researcher is different from information provided by measurement process -- Ex. people tell researcher they use Snapchat but they do not
  • Sampling error: when sample does not represent the target population - ex. Original people to be interviewed did not show-up OR refused to cooperate.
~~ Probability Samples
Simple Random Sample Every member of the population has a known and equal chance of selection
Stratified sample Divide the population into mutually exclusive groups (ex. gender, age) then randomly sample clusters.
Cluster sample Divide the population into mutually exclusive groups (ex. geographic are) then the researcher selects data from all of the elements or from a probability sample of all elements in each sample
Systematic Sample A list of the population is obtained and a skip interval is obtained by dividing the sample by the population size. The beginning number is randomly chosen.
~~ Nonprobability Samples
Convenience Sample The researcher selects the easiest population members from which to obtain information
Judgement Sample The researcher's selection criteria are based on personal judgement that th elements chosen will likely give accurate information
Quota Sample The researcher finds a prescribed number of people in several categories so that each group has a specific number represented
Snowball Sample Additional respondents are selected on the basis of referrals from initial respondents. This method is used when a desired type of respondent is difficult to find.
